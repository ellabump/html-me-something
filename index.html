  1. Parque Nacional de Patagonia

    2. The far south of Chile holds jagged peaks and glacial winds. I wanted to explore a territory that was wild, untamed. In November 2015, the start of the southern hemisphere's summer, I volunteered in Patagonia at a national park in construction, tearing down fences from old estancias and removing invasive species. This is the website for Conservacion Patagonica.

  2. Glaciers

    3. I had planned to go work for a rafting company across the border of Argentina, in Mendoza but after glimpsing the mountains around me I couldn't resist the far south where the coastline split in fjords, and the most famous mountains reigned.

  3. The Lemon Farm, Santiago

    4. I returned to the city of Santiago in January, which houses forty percent of Chile's population. From there, I worked at a lemon farm, a permaculture farm, and traveled to the Futaleufu with my amazing friend Lydia Bleifuss.

Great book by Bruce Chatwin: "In Patagonia"
