  1. Parque Nacional de Patagonia

    2. My gaze after college was drawn south, to jagged peaks and glacial winds. I wanted to explore a territory that was wild, untamed. I thought I'd try for Patagonia. I got into a volunteer program filled with physical labor; we'd be tearing down fences from old estancias and removing invasive species. Conservationists Kris and Doug Thompkins had privately bought land and were turning it into a park, saving an ecosystem that was not well protected by the Chilean government. I spent three weeks in the field.
